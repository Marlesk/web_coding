Projects

Dynamic Borrowing through Empirical Power Priors for Binomial Data

Master's Thesis

October 2019 – July 2020

https://pergamos.lib.uoa.gr/uoa/dl/frontend/el/browse/2927758

Bayesian methods can mitigate the borrowing of historical data through power prior distribution. Power prior distributions are a useful tool for incorporation data from one or more historical studies. However, the incorporation of historical data can increase the type I error. The idea of dynamic lending is to recognize when current data appears to be incompatible with historical data. A recent publication deals with the aforementioned issue in an empirical Bayes manner, taking into account the discrepancy between past and current data, with type I error as a ruler. The method is described for continuous, normally distributed data. Extension to the case of dichotomous outcomes was the goal of this thesis. We extended the methodology for Bayesian hypothesis testing using posterior probabilities. The method was employed in re-designing a clinical trial from the field of oncology. We used R for programming and coding, utilizing simulation and Bayesian methods to extend this methodology to clinical trials (Phase I, II, and III).
